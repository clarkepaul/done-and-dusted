Kids bunk bed

Aug 2019

I'm building a bunk bed for the kids above the main fixed double bed. We're keeping the sleeping areas in the back so we can still use the dinning area as normal in the evenings. I want the bunk to be easily stored away when not in use so its going to fold away into the wall like a Murphy Bed.

I've purchased a foldup bed from Trademe which I'll be using for the bunk. For it to fit I've had to take away the cupboards above the main double bed to make enough space and I'll also be moving the TV to the other side of the bus (I've removed it all together for now).

Next will be to create a gaurd rail to stop Isaac fallong out of the bed.

GPS tracker installation

Sept 2019

I've been pretty casual with parking my bus in places which aren't the safest, for a little bit more piece of mind I've purchased a GPS tracker for the bus.

It's a fairly simple device which will alert me if it moves, or by sending it a SMS it will let me know its location.

Purchased off AliExpress for about $30, so far its been easy to setup. I have it being tracked on a map, it has it's own battery incase its disconnected from the bus battery.

Bed headboard

Oct 2019

Because there's a bit of a gap between the double mattress and the back wall I've made a headboard to fill the gap. It's an old foam mattress which makes for a pretty comfy headboard and provides and extra thermal layer between the sleeping area and the back of the bus.

Kim had some fabric lying around which I've used to cover the foam and I had a board up in the attic to give the headboard some strentgh. I sliced the mattress up with an electric bread knife to form the shape, with special attention to ensure the bunk still goes up and down by splitting the headboard into a main piece and a smaller peice which can be used if the bunk is up or put away if the bunk is down.

I might splash out at some stage to purchase some thick wool felt material to cover it but for now there's more important things to do.
