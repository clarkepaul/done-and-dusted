The adventure begins

Our bus was bought as a weekend wanderer in July 2019. Bought to increase the good times together, reduce stress, and spend more time out and about in nature.

Dusty is a 1980 wide body 7.2m Ford truck with a bus shell. We chose a bus so that we could be more hands on with customising and fitting it out. There's enough of space for the kids to grow into and a decent enough living space for those longer trips. One of the biggest selling points was the seating arrangement where we could all ride up the front together. We've fitted additional kids beds, and adding a few of our own touches but its mostly good to go as is.

I think we've gone for the right bus size for our family. Having permanent dinning and sleeping areas has been a real game changer compared to our previous caravan, rather than having to reconfigering things for kids naps, and connect water/waste tanks each time.

We bought it fully kitted out with all of the basics we need for the time being.

It came with; kitchen, fridge, oven, gas hot water, toilet, shower, solar and alternator (12v/24v), swivel 22 inch TV, LED lights throughout, stainless grey/fresh tanks (200 ltrs), 4 berth with full seatbelts, double bed, separate dinning area, towbar, awning, large stand up boot.

We have since removed the T.V. and some cupboards, added some kids beds, and added some more lighting. More on the changes are on the Tinkering page.

It was a passenger coach up to about 2007 (owned by several tour companies), we are the third private use owner but most of the motorhome conversion was done by the previous owner.

It used to be called Done and Dusted by the previous owners and we kept it like that for a couple years. It's spent most of its life in Auckland but has previously done a stint in Wellington several years ago.

At some some stage the front of the bus has been changed for fibreglass, maybe it was damaged or it was done at the time of the engine swap? it's a shame it's lost a bit of it original character.

A little more about it's history.

It's registered in NZ as a DOG10 model but as far as I can tell that model never existed. I think it's more likely a D0610 which looks very similar or maybe a D0710.

Days gone by...

We previously owned a small caravan which took us around the East Cape of the North Island, and many weekends away camping.

Owning a caravan gave us a taste of what to expect on the road and we wanted more of it. We loved the time we had with it but with another addition to the family we appreciate the extra space of the bus.
